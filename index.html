About Us

Built for Nigerians,
by Nigerians

We believe every Nigerian deserves a clear picture of their finances. PennyTree was born out of the frustration of juggling multiple bank apps and never really knowing where the money goes. We're on a mission to make personal finance simple, visual, and empowering for everyone.

Clarity

We cut through the noise. One dashboard, all your accounts, zero confusion. Your money, crystal clear.

Security

Bank-level encryption and read-only access. We never store your bank credentials — your data stays yours.

Empowerment

Knowledge is power. We give you the insights and tools to make better financial decisions every day.

Community

Finance doesn't have to be lonely. We're building a community of Nigerians who care about their money.

Innovation

We leverage AI and modern tech to make features that feel magical — while keeping things simple.

Impact

Every feature we build is designed to help Nigerians save more, spend smarter, and achieve financial freedom.

Founder's Story

The Story Behind PennyTree

Tega Oboraruvwe
CEO & Founder
Tega Oboraruvwe
CEO & Founder, PennyTree
The Problem

"I had four bank accounts and I could never tell you how much I'd spent in a month. I'd check one app, then another, then try to add things up on a calculator. There had to be a better way."

The Spark

"I looked at what people in other countries had — apps like Mint and YNAB. But nothing existed that truly understood the Nigerian banking experience. The multiple accounts, the POS payments, the transfers."

Building PennyTree

"So I started building. Every feature was inspired by conversations with real Nigerians about their money struggles. We obsessed over making it simple — because finance apps shouldn't need a manual."

Today

"Thousands of Nigerians now use PennyTree to understand and control their finances. This is just the beginning — we're building the financial companion every Nigerian deserves."

"Every naira you understand is a naira you control. That's the heart of PennyTree — turning financial confusion into clarity."
